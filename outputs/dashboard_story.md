# Dashboard Story

## Executive Summary
Across 4,200 orders and two years of data (Jan 2024–Dec 2025), the business generated ₹21.70 Cr in sales at a 15.35% profit margin. Overall performance is healthy and stable, but it masks two compounding problems — Furniture's weak margins and uncontrolled discounting above 25% — sitting alongside one clear, underused opportunity: the Organic acquisition channel. The dashboard is built to make leadership see these patterns at a glance rather than buried in 4,200 individual rows.

## What Is Performing Well
Technology is the engine of the business — 71% of sales at an 18.2% margin, the highest of any category. The South region leads on volume, and Rajasthan alone punches far above its weight (38% of North region's total sales at a 15.8% margin). The Organic campaign channel is the standout efficiency story: it drives 40% of all orders and the second-highest average order value of any channel, with zero acquisition spend — it's outperforming Paid traffic on both volume and order value. Same Day shipping, while a small share of orders, delivers the fastest turnaround and the lowest return rate of any shipping mode.

## What Is Underperforming
Furniture is the clearest weak spot: it's the second-largest category by sales but converts that revenue into only a 6.9% margin — less than half the company average — and every single Furniture sub-category sits below 8.5% margin. It also carries the highest return rates in the dataset (Bookcases, Furnishings, and Chairs all above 8%, roughly 2–3x Technology's return rates). A handful of states — Maharashtra and Uttar Pradesh in particular — have decent order volume but margins meaningfully below their peers.

## What Risks Are Visible
The sharpest risk is discount governance: profit margin falls from 20.6% at no discount to -5.0% at 35% discount, and orders discounted 25% or more account for 87% of all loss-making orders in the dataset. This isn't a gradual slope — it's a cliff. That risk is concentrated almost entirely in one place: 93% of all loss-making orders are Furniture. In other words, the business has one category, getting discounted past a threshold where it stops making money, repeatedly. Separately, Home Office is the largest customer segment by sales but has a 45% higher return rate than Consumer or Corporate — a cost that doesn't show up directly in the profit numbers but erodes the segment's real value.

## What Opportunities Are Visible
The Organic channel is a proven, low-cost growth lever that's already working — the data supports investing further in it (SEO, content, referral programs) rather than shifting more budget to Paid acquisition. On the regional side, the margin efficiency seen in Jharkhand, Karnataka, and Haryana suggests there's a transferable playbook (pricing, product mix, or operational practice) that could be tested in lower-margin states like Maharashtra.

## Recommended Business Actions
1. Cap Furniture discounts below 25%, with a tighter ceiling than other categories given its already-thin margins.
2. Require manager approval for any discount above 20% company-wide — the data shows this is where margin protection should activate.
3. Open a root-cause investigation into Home Office returns specifically, since it's the largest segment with the most value at stake.
4. Increase investment in Organic-channel growth (content/SEO) rather than scaling Paid spend further.
5. Study Rajasthan's and Jharkhand's product/pricing mix as a template for underperforming states like Maharashtra and Uttar Pradesh.
6. Commission a Furniture-specific quality and packaging audit to address the elevated return rate there.

## Limitations of the Dashboard
- The dataset covers two fiscal years only; it cannot confirm whether patterns (e.g. the Aug 2025 sales spike) are seasonal, one-off, or part of a longer trend.
- Profit figures reflect the original sale only — they do not net out the cost of processing returns, so segments/categories with high return rates (Furniture, Home Office) are likely even less profitable in practice than the raw numbers show.
- Campaign channel and customer rating fields have a small number of missing values (24 and 32 records respectively, under 1% of the dataset), which is unlikely to change conclusions but is worth noting.
- The data does not include unit cost or supplier information, so it's not possible to tell whether Furniture's low margin is a pricing problem, a cost problem, or both.
- Geographic analysis is at the state level; city-level patterns are not explored in this version of the dashboard.

## Suggested Next Analysis
- A cohort or time-series breakdown of the Aug 2025 sales spike to identify whether it's repeatable.
- A request for unit cost data to separate Furniture's pricing strategy from its underlying cost structure.
- A return-reason analysis (if such data can be collected) to move from "Furniture and Home Office return more" to "why."
- A discount-elasticity study to find the actual profit-maximizing discount ceiling per category, rather than a single company-wide cap.
